Journey Timeline

Phase by Phase

Every moment of your mission is carefully orchestrated for maximum safety and experience.

Phase 01

Pre-Flight Preparation

72 hours before launch, passengers report to the Exodus Spaceport. Medical evaluations, zero-G familiarization, and mission briefings ensure you're ready for departure.

Duration: 3 Days
Phase 02

Boarding & Launch

Secure your harness in your assigned cabin class. The plasma ion drives ignite, delivering 3.2G of acceleration as you break free from Earth's gravity well.

Duration: 45 Minutes
Phase 03

Orbital Assembly

In low Earth orbit, the vessel links with the transit module. The full ship assembles — living quarters, observation decks, and the centrifugal gravity ring engage.

Duration: 6 Hours
Phase 04

Trans-Mars Injection

The main engines fire in a precise burn, sending you on a Hohmann transfer orbit toward Mars. From this point, you're truly on your way.

Duration: 2 Hours
Phase 05

Deep Space Transit

137 days of interplanetary cruise. Enjoy the amenities, attend skill workshops, exercise in the gravity ring, and watch the cosmos from observation lounges.

Duration: 137 Days
Phase 06

Mars Orbit Insertion

Deceleration burns slow the vessel for Mars orbital capture. You'll orbit Mars for 24 hours as final landing preparations are made.

Duration: 24 Hours
Phase 07

Descent & Landing

The landing shuttle detaches and begins atmospheric entry. Heat shields glow orange before the retro-rockets fire, settling you gently onto the Martian surface.

Duration: 90 Minutes
Phase 08

Colony Integration

Welcome to Olympus Colony. New arrivals undergo acclimatization, receive habitat assignments, and begin orientation for life on the Red Planet.

Duration: 7 Days
